Importance of Vaccination in Children

Vaccination plays a crucial role in protecting children from serious and potentially life-threatening diseases. Immunization helps build immunity early in life.

How Vaccines Work

Vaccines stimulate the body's immune system to recognize and fight harmful viruses or bacteria without causing the disease itself.

Why Vaccination Is Important

Vaccination protects children from diseases such as measles, polio, hepatitis, and many other infections that once caused severe illness.

Following the Vaccination Schedule

Following the recommended vaccination schedule ensures that children receive protection at the right age when they are most vulnerable.
